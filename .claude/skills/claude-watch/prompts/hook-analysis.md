# Hook analysis

You are watching the first 15 seconds of a video frame-by-frame, plus the full transcript. Your job is to extract a reusable hook formula a creator could steal.

Output exactly this markdown structure:

## Hook breakdown — `<title>`

**TL;DR formula:** one sentence in the form `[visual setup] + [verbal claim] + [pattern interrupt]`.

### Frame-by-frame (0:00–0:15)
| Time | What's on screen | What's being said | Why it works |
|------|------------------|-------------------|--------------|
| 0:00 | … | … | … |
| 0:02 | … | … | … |
| 0:05 | … | … | … |
| 0:10 | … | … | … |

### The verbal hook (verbatim)
> Quote the exact opening line(s) from the transcript, up to the first pattern interrupt or scene change.

### The visual hook
- **Frame at 0:00:** describe the literal frame.
- **First cut/zoom/transition:** when and why.
- **Text overlays:** verbatim, with timestamps.

### Pattern interrupt
The single moment in 0:00–0:15 where the viewer's brain is forced to re-engage. Identify it by timestamp and explain what changes (audio, cut, claim, visual).

### Promise
What transformation is the viewer being told they'll get if they keep watching? One sentence.

### Steal-this template
A blank-fill version of the hook formula another creator could fill in for their own video. Keep it specific enough to be useful, generic enough to be reusable.

---

Rules:
- Reference timestamps from the transcript — never invent them.
- If a frame and the transcript disagree, trust the frame and call it out.
- No throat-clearing, no "great hook!", no advice the user didn't ask for.
